I.
The Market

Two-and-a-half trillion dollars sits in the global trade finance gap.

And it widens with every tariff, every new compliance regime, every supply chain rerouted by geopolitics.

$2.5T
Global trade finance gap — the unmet demand for working-capital instruments behind international commerce.
41%
Of trade finance requests from small- and mid-sized businesses are rejected outright by conventional banks.
~10%
Of global merchandise trade goes unfinanced — a structural shortfall, not a cyclical one.

Conventional banks have pulled back. Compliance regimes have thickened. Emerging-market commerce has grown faster than institutional risk appetite. The result is a structural shortage of working capital at precisely the points where global commerce now happens — the cross-border, the cross-currency, the underbanked, the urgent. CBR Trade Ltd was built for that gap. We structure the deals institutional finance won't, in the jurisdictions it can't, on the timeline it wishes it could.

Source · Asian Development Bank, Global Trade Finance Gap Survey, 2025

II.
The Studio

Composition is the discipline. Trade is the canvas.

An artist's instincts — for structure, for restraint, for what is left out — applied for twenty-five years to the architecture of international commerce.

Pl. i — Studio

CBR Trade Ltd was founded by Caren Raphael — a working artist whose second practice has, over twenty-five years, become one of the more quietly consequential trade-finance operations in private hands. The same instincts that govern her studio — composition, restraint, an eye for what others overlook — govern her approach to letters of credit, purchase-order finance, and the choreography of international commerce.

The firm structures capital for importers, exporters, manufacturers, distributors, and sovereign clients across four continents. It has moved gold and diamonds, gas turbines and rice, personal protective equipment in the worst weeks of a pandemic and clean water into regions that had none. Each engagement is approached the way an artist approaches a canvas: deliberately, with intention, and with attention to the whole composition.

III.
The Approach

Four principles. No exceptions.

A practice built on what we choose not to do, as much as what we do.

01
Composition over scale.
Every engagement is structured as bespoke architecture, never as a product to be sold. We accept fewer engagements so that each one is structured with care — and so that none compromises the rest.
02
Relationship over volume.
We work with a deliberately limited number of clients. Every transaction is led by a principal who knows the counterparty by name, by jurisdiction, and by track record.
03
Multi-jurisdiction by design.
Eight offices across four continents — from Lagos to Tel Aviv, Accra to São Paulo. We originate where the deal lives, not from a remote desk in a single financial capital.
04
Patient capital, decisive execution.
We wait for the right structure, never the easy one. But once a structure is right, we move at the speed international commerce actually requires — which is faster than most institutions can.
IV.
Capabilities

A working palette.

Six core instruments. Each deployed only where it is the right one — and only after the underlying composition is understood.

i.
Letter of Credit Structuring
Origination, deployment, and management of LC facilities across multiple jurisdictions and currencies — including the structures conventional banks no longer offer.
ii.
Purchase Order Finance
Pre-shipment capital structured to bridge importers and exporters at the moment of commitment — the point at which most working-capital gaps actually open.
iii.
Accounts Receivable Finance
Post-shipment liquidity that turns invoices into working capital without disturbing the underlying customer relationship.
iv.
Credit Lines & Capital Raises
Origination of bank and private facilities for operating businesses, importers, and distributors — including capital for growth-stage companies banks have not yet learned to underwrite.
v.
Global Sourcing & Fulfillment
End-to-end sourcing, contracting, logistics, and delivery for buyers operating in time-critical markets — including crisis-response procurement at sovereign scale.
vi.
Banking Facility Arrangement
Structuring of multi-million-dollar banking facilities for exporters of complex industrial equipment, including energy, machinery, and infrastructure.
